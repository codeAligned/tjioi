What is TJIOI?

The Thomas Jefferson Intermediate Open in Informatics is a high school programming competition hosted by the Senior Computer Team at TJHSST. Each contestant will compete in a group of four and participate in the theoretical and programming rounds. The competition consists of a wide variety of problem difficulties suitable for both computer science beginners and veterans. Prizes and awards will be given to the highest scoring teams. Past Contests

Purpose

Our purpose is to spread interest in computer science to high school students in the local area through challenging problems found beyond the classroom.
